12. FAQ & Troubleshooting

info

Markd is built with aggressive error-handling. If something goes wrong, the app is designed to catch it, halt the operation, and tell you exactly why.

Q: I deleted or renamed a folder directly in Chrome. What happens to the app?​

A: Markd tracks your folders by their hidden, internal Chrome IDs, making it very resilient to external changes!

  • If you rename in Chrome: The ID remains the same. When you click Find & Refresh Plan, the app notices the name change and seamlessly updates your Blueprint without breaking anything.
  • If you delete in Chrome: The ID is destroyed. When you open the app, it notices the folder is gone and turns it Red (Missing) in your list. It preserves the folder in your Blueprint as a ghost, giving you the choice to either delete it from your plan or restore it.

Q: Why do I get a "Folder Exists in the browser" warning when trying to move or create a folder?​

A: This specific dialog is an "Orphan Collision" warning. It triggers when you try to interact with a folder that the app thinks is just a blueprint (Blue or Red), but Markd suddenly discovers that a real, physical folder with that exact same name was secretly created in Chrome while the app wasn't looking.
To prevent duplicating folders or overwriting your data, Markd halts your operation and asks you to click Sync & Cancel Operation. This forces the app to safely link your Blueprint to that newly discovered physical folder, allowing you to safely retry your move.

note

No data will be lost using this method.

Q: Why can't I rename an orange "Naming Conflict" folder in the app?​

A: Naming Conflicts occur when Markd detects two physical folders in Chrome with the exact same name. Because Markd doesn't know which one contains the correct bookmarks, the folder is locked in the UI.
The Fix: You cannot rename a conflict inline. You must either click the Trash icon to delete the conflicted definition, or click the Apply Sync Plan button in the sidebar. The Sync Engine will automatically append a number (e.g., [1]) to the duplicate and physically resolve it in Chrome.

Q: My AI sorting suddenly stopped and said "Rate Limit Reached." What happened?​

A: The AI provider you selected (e.g., Groq or OpenAI) has temporarily blocked Markd for sending too many requests too quickly.
The Fix: Markd handles this gracefully. It will log a yellow ⚠️ Skipped warning for that bookmark and move to the next one. Wait a minute for your API limits to reset, and run the sort again.

Q: I tried to connect to Ollama, but it says "Connection Refused."​

A: This means Markd cannot see your local AI server.
The Fix:

  1. Ensure the Ollama application is actually running on your computer.
  2. Check that the URL in Settings matches exactly (usually http://localhost:11434).
  3. If it connects but fails to sort bookmarks, go to Settings and check the box for "Single Prompt Mode". Some smaller models crash on conversational formatting, and this forces Markd to use a simpler /api/generate command.

Q: The app suddenly locked me out and everything says "Offline".​

A: Markd performs a lightweight, background validation check every 2 hours. If your computer disconnects from the Wi-Fi, or if a firewall blocks the check, the app instantly enters Offline Lockdown.
The Fix: Reconnect to the internet and click the Reconnect / Verify button on the orange banner at the bottom of the sidebar. Markd will restore your session without needing to reload the page.

Q: I accidentally deleted my "Other Bookmarks" folder in Chrome, and now Markd won't load properly!​

A: You triggered a Root Corruption. Markd detected that its home base no longer exists in Chrome.
The Fix: Markd will intercept the startup sequence and automatically force open the Settings modal. Look for the red warning text under "Root Folder Location," and use the dropdown to select a new valid home (such as the Bookmarks Bar). Click Save, and the app will repair itself.

Q: Why are some links marked red in the Activity Log with ERR_CODE:403 or ERR_CODE:404?​

A: During AI sorting, Markd's scraper visits the URL to read the text.

  • 404 Not Found: The website is dead. The AI will skip it.
  • 403 / CORS: The website has a strict firewall (like Cloudflare) that blocks bots. Markd will skip it and log a yellow warning, leaving the bookmark safely where it is.
  • 0 / Network Error: Your internet dropped while trying to read the page. Markd will immediately halt the entire sorting process to protect your data.
