3. Workspace Navigation

1. The Top Bar Search & Controls​

At the very top of the workspace, you have global controls for navigating your bookmarks:

  • Global Search (Button): Clicking "Search" opens a modal that searches every bookmark in your current root. It scores results by relevance (Exact match -> Starts with -> Contains) and searches both the Bookmark Title and the URL. Clicking a result opens it in a new tab.
  • Structure Filter (Input): Typing in the "Filter structure..." box performs a live, local filter of the Category Tree on your screen. It hides non-matching folders, highlights your search term in Indigo, and automatically expands parent folders to reveal matching subcategories. Press Escape or click the X to clear the filter.
    Clicking on an item from the matching ones switches the Filter mode to the normal category view, showing the selected item and its surroundings.
  • Expand/Collapse All ( , , , ): Two buttons exist here — one for Main Folders, one for Bookmarks. Clicking them will smoothly snap open or close every valid list on your screen. These buttons next to folders and bookmarks only appear if there is actually content inside.
  • Smart Selection Filters: A row of specific checkboxes (Main Folders, Subfolders, Empty Folders, Main Orphans, Suborphans, Flat Proposals, Naming Conflicts).
    • Strict Mode Locking: When you click one of these boxes (e.g., Empty Folders), Markd enters a "locked" selection mode. It will instantly select all empty folders, and disable the checkboxes for every other type of folder to prevent you from mixing incompatible operations.
  • Theme Toggle ( / / ): Cycles the app's appearance between System, Light, and Dark modes. When set to System, the app will smoothly and automatically sync with your computer's OS color settings in real-time.
  • Help ( ): Re-opens the quick-start onboarding guide.

2. The Sidebar Status Panel & In-App Help​

Markd is designed with a deeply integrated help system. You never have to guess what a button does or what state the app is in.

The Sidebar Status Panel At the top of the left sidebar, a live status panel displays the exact technical state of your workspace:

  • Plan: Shows your current tier (Free, Trial, or Premium).
  • API Key / Provider / Model: Shows exactly which AI engine is actively routing your bookmarks. If your internet drops, these instantly switch to Disabled / Offline.
  • Auto-Cat.: Shows your current sorting rules for auto categorization (e.g., "Create New" vs "Existing").
  • Root Location: Shows the exact physical Chrome folder Markd is currently managing.

Tooltips & Dialogs Almost every element in Markd has a rich tooltip. If you are ever confused, simply hover your mouse over an item.

  • Hovering over a Folder Badge (e.g., [12 | 3]) reveals a detailed breakdown: "12 bookmarks total (4 in subfolders). Direct: 8. Total subfolders: 3."
  • Hovering over a Disabled Button will tell you exactly why it is disabled (e.g., "Clear your folder selection to select bookmarks").

Interactive Toasts & Activity Log

  • Toasts: Temporary notifications appear in the top right. If you hover your mouse over a Toast, it will stay on the screen indefinitely so you can read it. It fades out 1 second after your mouse leaves, or instantly if you click the 'X'.
  • The Activity Log: Located at the bottom, click on its header or the to slide it open. This log records every action (e.g., folder creations, errors).
    Preview Mode relies heavily on this log — if you run an AI Preview, the log will populate with exactly where the AI would have moved your bookmarks without actually moving them. The "Clear" buttons clears the log.

3. Contextual Dialogs & Safety Warnings​

When Markd needs your input or confirmation, it doesn't just show a generic "Are you sure?" popup. The dialog system is designed to be highly descriptive, context-aware, and user-friendly, ensuring you always know exactly what is happening to your data.

Smart, Info-Oriented Dialogs
Normal dialogs are designed to guide you through complex decisions by providing all the necessary information upfront:

  • Contextual Details: If you try to delete a folder, the dialog won't just ask for confirmation; it will explicitly tell you the folder's name and exactly how many bookmarks are inside it.
  • Actionable Options: Instead of a simple "Yes/No", dialogs offer smart routing. For example, if you delete a folder full of bookmarks, the dialog gives you options to Move them to Root, Move them to the Parent folder, or Ignore & Delete.
  • Conflict Resolution: If you drag a folder into a destination where the name already exists, the dialog will explain the conflict and offer targeted solutions like Merge, Overwrite, or Rename & Move.

Danger Dialogs
For highly destructive or sensitive actions, the app uses a visually distinct Danger Theme.

  • Visual Warning: These dialogs instantly grab your attention with a deep red background, red text, and a icon in the header, preventing accidental clicks.
  • Honest Irreversibility: Markd only tells you an action "cannot be undone" when it is strictly true (such as wiping your entire workspace, applying a sync plan to Chrome, or restoring a backup). If you are simply deleting an empty folder or a single bookmark, the Danger Dialog will still warn you with the red theme, but it omits the "cannot be undone" text—because you can simply use the app's Undo button to bring it right back!
  • The Ultimate Safety Net: Every Danger Dialog that involves deleting folders includes a clear reminder: Protected System Folders ( ) are completely safe and will never be deleted, even during bulk wipe operations.
