5. Managing Bookmarks

While the main category tree helps you organize your folders, the actual links live inside the Bookmark Drawers. Click the bookmark expander icon on any active folder to slide open its drawer and manage the links inside.

  • Recent Bookmark Indicator ( ): Any bookmark that was added to your browser within the last 7 days will automatically display a icon next to its name. This helps you quickly identify fresh content that might need sorting, without having to dig through your lists.

1. Hover Actions (Edit, Copy, Delete)​

To keep the interface clean, bookmark controls are hidden until you need them. Hover your mouse over any individual bookmark row to reveal the quick-action icons on the right:

  • Edit Title or URL ( ): Clicking this instantly transforms the bookmark row into an inline text editor. You can fix typos in the title or update a broken URL directly. Press Enter to save your changes, or Escape to cancel and discard them.
  • Copy URL ( ): Instantly copies the website's link to your clipboard.
  • Delete ( ): Permanently removes the bookmark from Chrome (after a quick confirmation prompt).

2. The Context Menu (Right-Click)​

If you prefer using your mouse, simply Right-Click on any bookmark to open a custom Context Menu. From here, you have access to the same Edit, Copy, and Delete actions, plus two handy shortcuts:

  • Open in New Tab: Opens the website in the background without closing Markd.
  • Open in New Window: Launches the site in a completely separate Chrome window.

3. Finding Bookmarks: Local Filters vs. Global Search​

If you have a massive collection of links, Markd provides two different ways to find what you are looking for:

  • Local Filter (Inside the Drawer): At the top of every open bookmark drawer is a specific filter box. As you type, it instantly hides bookmarks that don't match, allowing you to quickly sift through large folders.
    • Smart Placeholder: The text box dynamically updates based on what's inside. If a folder has 15 links, it says "Filter 15 bookmarks..." If you've narrowed it down (or only have one link left), it intelligently changes to "Search 1 bookmark..."
  • Global Search (Top Bar): If you don't know which folder a bookmark is hiding in, click the Search button at the very top-left of the app. This opens a modal that searches every bookmark in your workspace. It scores results by relevance and highlights exactly why it matched (whether it found your search term in the Title or hidden inside the URL).

4. Bulk Selection: The "Select All" Checkbox​

Next to the filter box at the top of every drawer is a Select All checkbox.

  • Clicking this will instantly select every bookmark currently visible in that drawer, triggering the Floating Action Bar (FAB) at the bottom of your screen for bulk moving or deleting.
  • Smart Filtering: If you type "Recipe" into the filter box, the drawer might narrow down from 100 bookmarks to just 5. If you click Select All while a filter is active, it will only select those 5 visible bookmarks, protecting the other 95 from being accidentally modified!
  • Folder Mutual Exclusivity: Remember, you cannot select Folders and Bookmarks at the same time. If you try to click a bookmark's checkbox while a folder is selected, Markd will block the click and show a friendly reminder to clear your folder selection first.

5. The "Check Broken Links" Button​

Also located in the header of every bookmark drawer is the Check Broken Links button. Instead of scanning your entire workspace, clicking this button performs a targeted, lightning-fast scan on only the bookmarks inside that specific folder. It will flag any dead websites by turning them Red, so you can easily delete or edit them.

6. Bulk Actions & The Floating Action Bar (FAB)​

When you select one or more bookmarks (using individual checkboxes or the "Select All" feature), the Floating Action Bar (FAB) smoothly slides up from the bottom of the workspace.

This bar acts as your command center for bulk operations. It displays a live count of exactly how many bookmarks you have selected and from how many different folders. From the FAB, you can perform the following actions:

  • Move to...: Opens a clean, searchable popup window displaying your entire category tree. Simply click a destination folder, and Markd will instantly move the entire batch.
tip

If you select a Blue (Plan) or Red (Missing) "Orphan" folder as the destination, the app is smart enough to physically create that folder in Chrome first, before moving your bookmarks safely inside!

  • Move to Root: (This button only appears if your selected bookmarks are currently inside folders). Clicking this will extract the selected bookmarks out of their specific folders and place them loosely at the very top level of your active workspace (e.g., directly in "Other Bookmarks"). This is a great way to quickly "un-sort" items.
  • Copy URLs: Extracts the web addresses from all selected bookmarks and copies them to your clipboard as a neat, multi-line list. This is perfect if you need to share a batch of links with a coworker or friend in an email or chat.
  • Delete: Permanently removes the selected batch of bookmarks from your browser. (Don't worry, a danger confirmation dialog will appear to ensure you don't delete them accidentally).
  • Clear: Instantly deselects all bookmarks, clears the checkboxes, and hides the FAB. Pressing the Escape key on your keyboard does this as well.

Bulk Drag & Drop​

You aren't restricted to just the buttons on the FAB! Markd fully supports bulk drag-and-drop.

Once you have multiple bookmarks checked, simply click and drag any of the selected rows. The FAB will temporarily hide, and the app will bundle your selection into a single visual badge (e.g., "☰ 5 Bookmarks"). You can then drop the entire batch into a new folder in one swift motion.
